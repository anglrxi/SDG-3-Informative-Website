Overview
SDG 3 aims to guarantee that every individual, regardless of age, gender, or background, has the opportunity to live a healthy life and achieve overall well-being. Health is a multifaceted concept—it is not merely the absence of illness but encompasses physical, mental, and social well-being. This goal emphasizes both prevention and treatment: preventing diseases through vaccination, education, and healthy lifestyle choices, while also ensuring timely and effective treatment for those who are ill.

Mental health is a crucial component, recognizing that psychological well-being is just as important as physical health. SDG 3 also focuses on equitable access to healthcare services, ensuring that communities worldwide—especially those in low-income or underserved regions—can obtain essential medicines, quality care, and health infrastructure.

Beyond healthcare services, creating environments that support health is vital. This includes promoting safe water and sanitation, healthy nutrition, reduced pollution, and conditions that foster physical activity and emotional resilience. By taking a holistic approach, SDG 3 aims to empower individuals and communities to lead longer, healthier, and more fulfilling lives, contributing to sustainable development globally.
